ECHO
RITUAL 💐

Interactive memorial experience for post-funeral emotional support.

Grief doesn't end after the funeral.
Support shouldn't either.

Role identity

# Ritual Space Concepting # Blender Hi-fi Rendering # Concept Development # Environmental Visualisation

PROJECT OVERVIEW

INSPIRATION

When someone close passes away, what remains is not only loss, but unfinished conversations, regret, and memories that still need a place to exist.

"The ritual ends before grief does."

PHENOMENON

After traditional funerals end, emotional support often fades quickly. People return to work, routines, and daily life while grief continues privately in the background.

Many unresolved feelings — regret, unfinished conversations, or unspoken memories — remain difficult to share within existing social and cultural structures.

BACKGROUND

Informed by Disenfranchised Grief theory (Doka, 1989), which highlights how grief can remain socially unsupported and unresolved.

THINKING...

How might we design an ongoing memorial experience that supports people beyond a single day of mourning?

PROBLEM SPACE

PROBLEM STATEMENT

The issue is not the absence of mourning rituals, but the lack of ongoing emotional space after formal rituals end.

INTERVIEW

"There were things I never got to tell them."

KEY INSIGHT

Grief often continues silently after formal rituals end. People need ongoing emotional spaces to express unresolved memories, regret, and continuing bonds.

DESIGN OPPORTUNITY

Design a slow ritual experience that supports reflection, memory, and reconnection beyond formal mourning.

DESIGN MOVES

DESIGN CONCEPT

A drifting ritual experience that supports unresolved grief through memory, reflection, and collective connection.

DESIGN TRANSITION

Rather than designing a single memorial object, we designed a slow emotional journey that allows grief to unfold over time.

EXPERIENCE COMPONENTS

  • Memory Kiosk — private reflection · voice / note / upload
  • Memory Wall — shared archive · continuing bonds
  • Gathering Table — collective remembrance, conversation, reconstruction

ITERATION FLOW

① Early Concept → ② Testing + Feedback → ③ Final Experience

FINAL OUTCOME

Echo Ritual transforms grieving into a slow spatial ritual. Visitors move through floating memorial spaces that support private reflection, shared remembrance, and collective reconnection. Rather than forcing closure, the experience gives grief a place to unfold over time.

KEY INTERACTION LOGIC

  1. Drifting spatial movement
  2. Breathing ambient lighting
  3. Memory upload and reflection
  4. Collective remembrance table
  5. Symbolic floating pathway

FUTURE DIRECTIONS

WHAT I DID

  • Researched unresolved grief through interviews and journey mapping.
  • Designed a ritual-based memorial experience focused on reflection and reconnection.
  • Developed spatial concepts, storyboards, iterations, and final visualisations.

WHAT I LEARNED

  • Atmosphere and pacing can be more meaningful than screen-based interaction.
  • User feedback strengthened the emotional direction of the project.
  • Research helped guide and justify design decisions.

WHAT I WOULD IMPROVE

  • Test the experience in a more physical and interactive environment.
  • Refine environmental details and interaction fidelity.
  • Develop a higher-fidelity embodied prototype.

HELENSBURGH
GLOW WORM
MICROSITE

Responsive ecological tourism platform for safer low-impact night exploration.

A responsive microsite helping visitors plan safer, low-impact visits to a fragile glow worm habitat.

Role identity

# UX Research # Information Architecture # Responsive Web Design # Vibe Coding

PROJECT OVERVIEW

INSPIRATION

The Glow Worm Tunnel is both a tourist destination and a fragile ecological habitat. Night visitors often lack clear guidance for safe and low-impact exploration.

LOCATION + HISTORY

Located near Helensburgh, NSW, the tunnel attracts photographers, hikers, and night explorers. Historic railway infrastructure later transformed into a glow worm habitat.

BACKGROUND

Visitors need accessible planning tools, environmental guidance, and clearer behavioural expectations before entering the tunnel.

THINKING...

How might we help visitors explore the tunnel safely while protecting a fragile glow worm colony?

PROBLEM SPACE

PROBLEM STATEMENT

Visitors often arrive without clear planning support, environmental guidance, or behavioural expectations.

USER NEEDS

  • Clear transport and access info
  • Real-time site status updates
  • Low-impact behaviour guidance
  • Confidence before night visits

KEY INSIGHTS

Visitors need faster trip planning, clear behavioural guidance, and confidence before arrival.

DESIGN OPPORTUNITY

  • Explore → site history + ecology
  • Visit → transport + planning
  • Guidelines → low-impact behaviour
  • Gallery → community updates + photos

DESIGN MOVES

DESIGN CONCEPT

Designing a low-impact digital exploration experience for a fragile natural environment.

KEY DESIGN DECISIONS

  • Move 01 — Dropdown Navigation: hover dropdowns let users access sub-pages quickly without overwhelming the main navigation.
  • Move 02 — Planning Support: search bar, embedded map, mini map, weather/status widgets.
  • Move 03 — Eco-behaviour Guidance: Do/Don't guidelines and CTA prompts toward low-impact behaviour.
  • Move 04 — Atmosphere as Interaction: animated firefly/glow effects create immersion and reinforce tunnel identity.

FINAL OUTCOME

A responsive microsite supporting safe, informed, and eco-conscious exploration of the Glow Worm Tunnel.

INTERFACE DETAILS

  • Real-time Visit Planning: weather / map / transport / status
  • Eco-safe Visitor Guidance: low-light rules / noise control / safety tips
  • Immersive Site Identity: dark UI / glow particles / tunnel textures

TOOLS: HTML + CSS

  • Responsive layouts
  • Hover dropdown navigation
  • Animated glow/firefly effects
  • Embedded maps and travel support
  • Structured information hierarchy

FUTURE DIRECTIONS

WHAT I DID

Designed and built a responsive microsite with information architecture, visual identity, navigation, map support, visitor guidelines, and front-end implementation.

WHAT I LEARNED

How web design can guide visitor behaviour by making ecological rules, planning information, and navigation easier to access.

WHAT I WOULD IMPROVE

Add live API data for weather/status, conduct usability testing with visitors, and further refine accessibility for mobile users.

"The future of exploration is not just discovery, but protection."

AI MUSIC
LEARNING SYSTEM
APP ♪

AI-assisted music learning experience focused on adaptive feedback and emotional engagement.

Practice is not only about accuracy.
Motivation and emotional support matter too.

Role identity

# UX Research # Interaction Design # Interface Prototyping # User Testing

PROJECT OVERVIEW

INSPIRATION

Many music learning apps focus only on technical correction, while motivation and emotional engagement are often ignored.

PHENOMENON

Advantages of AI-supported music learning include personalised learning paths, in-time feedback, accessibility & cost-effectiveness, and enhancing the fun of learning.

Disadvantages: tech dependency, limited accuracy in feedback, lack of human nuance.

BACKGROUND

With the rapid development of AI, its use in education is increasing. In music learning, however, AI is mostly used for drills and simple feedback.

THINKING...

How might AI support music learners through both real-time feedback and emotional engagement?

PROBLEM SPACE

PROBLEM STATEMENT

Existing music learning systems prioritise technical accuracy over emotional engagement and adaptive motivation.

INTERVIEW

"AI is creative and informative but lacks emotional depth." — Rose

"AI is not very good at human emotions." — Milikiauk

KEY INSIGHTS

Learners value emotional guidance and adaptive feedback during repetitive practice.

DESIGN OPPORTUNITY

Design a music learning experience that supports emotional engagement, adaptive pacing, and real-time guidance.

DESIGN MOVES

DESIGN CONCEPT

Reimagining AI as a collaborative music learning partner rather than a passive correction tool.

DESIGN TRANSITION

I started by sketching an AI-assisted practice app, then translated the concept into a clearer interaction system focused on feedback, motivation, and adaptive learning.

EXPERIENCE COMPONENTS

  1. Real-time Feedback — AI responds instantly to performance and mistakes.
  2. Emotional Motivation — rewards and visual feedback encourage continued practice.
  3. Adaptive Learning Path — practice adjusts to user progress and pacing.

ITERATION FLOW

① user plays music → ② AI analyses performance → ③ emotion + performance feedback → ④ adaptive practice suggestions → ⑤ user continues practice.

FINAL OUTCOME

An AI-supported music learning experience focused on feedback, motivation, and adaptive practice.

INTERFACE DETAILS

  • Library: users can browse instruments and explore music categories through a structured filtering system.
  • Practice: the screen combines sheet music, playback controls, and AI suggestions to support real-time learning feedback.
  • Furniture & Frames: editable home content from the shop helps users decorate their personalised homepage.

TOOLS

  • Figma — to prototype interface structure, interaction flow, and adaptive feedback systems.
  • AI-assisted ideation — to explore interaction concepts and emotional learning features during early development.

FUTURE DIRECTIONS

WHAT I DID

Designed and refined an AI-supported music learning experience focused on emotional engagement, adaptive feedback, and personalised practice.

WHAT I LEARNED

  • Users preferred encouraging feedback over strict correction.
  • Adaptive pacing increased engagement and motivation.
  • Emotional interaction made practice feel more personal.

WHAT I WOULD IMPROVE

With more time, I would further develop the adaptive feedback system and test the experience with real music learners over longer periods.
